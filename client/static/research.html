Company profiles

Click any card to expand. Ten companies we track closely.

Anthropic
Key model: Claude Sonnet 4.6
Funding: $74.8B across 15 rounds
Open source: No

Safety-focused AI lab. $350B valuation. Founded by former OpenAI VP of Research Dario Amodei and President Daniela Amodei. Unique governance: Long-Term Benefit Trust with escalating board control. Amazon ($8B) and Google ($2B+) are investors with zero voting rights.

OpenAI
Key model: GPT-5.2 / o4
Funding: $167.7B across 6 rounds
Open source: No

Pivoted from nonprofit to public benefit corporation. $730B pre-money valuation. Microsoft holds ~27%. Board reshaped after the Nov 2023 crisis that saw CEO Sam Altman briefly fired and reinstated within 5 days. Musk trial ongoing Mar 2026.

Google DeepMind
Key model: Gemini 3.1 Pro
Internal investment: $157.5B
Open source: No

World's largest AI lab. Merged Google Brain + DeepMind in 2023. Parent Alphabet is a $2.3T public company. Massive compute infrastructure advantage. Gemini models integrated across Google products.

Meta AI
Key model: LLaMA 4
Internal investment: $115B
Open source: Yes (LLaMA)

Open-source AI leader. LLaMA models are the most widely used open-weight foundation models. Yann LeCun departed Nov 2025. Meta's $1.6T market cap funds massive AI infrastructure buildout. The open-source strategy creates ecosystem lock-in without licensing revenue.

xAI
Key model: Grok 4
Funding: $30.5B across 4 rounds
Open source: Partial

Elon Musk's AI lab. Founded 2023. "Any lawful use" military stance contrasts with Anthropic's safety focus. Built the Memphis Supercluster (100K H100 GPUs). Grok integrated into X (Twitter) platform. $80B valuation.

DeepSeek
Key model: DeepSeek V3
Funding: Self-funded
Open source: Yes (MIT)

Self-funded open-source lab from China. Backed by quant fund High-Flyer. R1 rivaled GPT-4o at a fraction of the cost, shocking the industry. MIT-licensed models. No external venture funding. Operates under Chinese AI regulations.

Qwen (Alibaba)
Key model: Qwen 3.5
Internal investment: $63B
Open source: Yes (Apache)

Alibaba Cloud's AI division. Apache 2.0 licensed models. Strong multilingual capabilities. Part of Alibaba's cloud infrastructure play. Qwen2.5 models competitive with Western alternatives at significantly lower cost.

Perplexity
Key model: pplx-api
Funding: $1.6B across 7 rounds
Open source: No

AI-native search engine. $20B valuation. Founded by former Google/DeepMind researchers. Ongoing copyright disputes with publishers over content scraping. Growth-stage with 100M+ monthly queries.

Kimi (Moonshot AI)
Key model: Kimi 2.5
Funding: $2.6B across 5 rounds
Open source: No

Beijing-based lab. ~80 employees, $240M revenue. Known for long-context capabilities. Accused of training on Claude outputs. $10B valuation. Backed by Alibaba, Tencent, and other Chinese investors.

Mistral AI
Key model: Mistral Large
Funding: $3.2B across 4 rounds
Open source: Yes (Apache)

Europe's AI champion. French sovereignty angle. Founded by former DeepMind and Meta researchers. Open-weight model strategy. $15B valuation. EU AI Act compliance built in. Strong enterprise adoption in Europe.
