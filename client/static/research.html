Company profiles

Click any card to expand. Ten companies we track closely.

Anthropic
Key model: Claude Sonnet 4.6
Funding: $67.3B across 17 rounds
Open source: No

Safety-focused AI lab. $380B valuation (Feb 2026 Series G, $30B round). Founded by former OpenAI VP of Research Dario Amodei and President Daniela Amodei. Unique governance: Long-Term Benefit Trust with escalating board control. Amazon ($8B+) and Google ($2B+) are investors with zero voting rights. Annualised revenue ~$14B, with Claude Code alone at ~$2.5B ARR.

OpenAI
Key model: GPT-5.2 / o4
Funding: $122B (Mar 2026 round)
Open source: No

Pivoted from nonprofit to public benefit corporation. $852B post-money valuation (Mar 2026). Amazon ($50B), Nvidia ($30B), and SoftBank ($30B) led the latest round. Microsoft holds ~27%. Board reshaped after the Nov 2023 crisis. Most valuable startup in history.

Google DeepMind
Key model: Gemini 3.1 Pro
Parent market cap: $3.6T
Open source: No

World's largest AI lab. Merged Google Brain + DeepMind in 2023. Parent Alphabet is the 3rd most valuable public company globally. Massive compute infrastructure advantage. Gemini models integrated across Google products. Revenue (TTM) ~$403B, net income ~$132B.

Meta AI
Key model: LLaMA 4
Parent market cap: $1.5T
Open source: Yes (LLaMA)

Open-source AI leader. LLaMA models are the most widely used open-weight foundation models. Yann LeCun departed Nov 2025 to found AMI Labs after strategic disagreements with Zuckerberg over LLM direction. Meta Superintelligence Labs now led by Alexandr Wang (Scale AI CEO). The open-source strategy creates ecosystem lock-in without licensing revenue.

xAI
Key model: Grok 4
Valuation: $250B (SpaceX acquisition)
Open source: Partial

Elon Musk's AI lab. Founded 2023. Acquired by SpaceX in Feb 2026 at $250B valuation after raising $20B Series E in Jan 2026. Built the Memphis Supercluster (100K H100 GPUs). Grok integrated into X (Twitter) platform. "Any lawful use" military stance contrasts with Anthropic's safety focus.

DeepSeek
Key model: DeepSeek V3
Funding: Self-funded
Open source: Yes (MIT)

Self-funded open-source lab from China. Backed by quant fund High-Flyer, founded by Liang Wenfeng. R1 rivaled GPT-4o at a fraction of the cost, shocking the industry. MIT-licensed models. No external venture funding. Operates under Chinese AI regulations and US export controls on advanced chips.

Qwen (Alibaba)
Key model: Qwen 3.5
Internal investment: $63B
Open source: Yes (Apache 2.0)

Alibaba Cloud's AI division. Apache 2.0 licensed models with strong multilingual capabilities. Part of Alibaba's cloud infrastructure play. Qwen2.5 models competitive with Western alternatives at significantly lower cost. Free API tier available.

Perplexity
Key model: pplx-api
Funding: $1.7B across 11 rounds
Open source: No

AI-native search engine. $21B valuation (early 2026). Founded by former Google/DeepMind researchers. ~$200M ARR, targeting $656M in 2026. 780M+ monthly queries. Ongoing copyright disputes with publishers over content usage.

Kimi (Moonshot AI)
Key model: Kimi 2.5
Funding: $2.6B+ across 5+ rounds
Open source: No

Beijing-based lab. Known for long-context capabilities. Seeking $1B raise at $18B valuation (Mar 2026), up from $10B just months earlier. Backed by Alibaba, Tencent, and 5Y Capital. Accused of training on Claude outputs. The fastest valuation growth among Chinese AI startups.

Mistral AI
Key model: Mistral Large
Funding: $3.1B across 8 rounds
Open source: Yes (Apache)

Europe's AI champion. French sovereignty angle. ~862 employees. $14B valuation (Sep 2025). Secured €722M ($830M) debt financing in Mar 2026 for its first large-scale data centre near Paris with 13,800 Nvidia GB300 GPUs. EU AI Act compliance built in. Strong enterprise adoption in Europe.
