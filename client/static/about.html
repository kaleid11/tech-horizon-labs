Not all AI help is equal.

Three archetypes exist in the Australian AI consulting market. Knowing which one you're working with changes everything.

The Business Coach

Adds "AI tips" to existing business coaching programs. Not an AI practitioner. Uses one or two tools. Teaches ChatGPT prompting from a marketing lens. Can't build anything. Good for mindset, not implementation.

The Single-Tool Specialist

Deep expertise in one platform (usually ChatGPT or Copilot). Valuable for that tool, but their toolkit doesn't expand beyond it. The ACCC's action against Microsoft over Copilot lock-in is a live example of why single-tool dependence is a structural risk for Australian businesses.

The Frontier Orchestrator

Works across multiple AI platforms (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Agent Opus), builds real applications, and translates frontier capabilities into business outcomes. The right tool for each task, not the one your vendor bundles.

Huxley Peckham is the third. He's built PaddockMap — a land parcel intelligence tool — using Claude Code and Replit over a weekend while the government's equivalent project spent $3.5M. Every week he's running new models in production, not watching YouTube videos about them.
